UX Problem Identification

Problem: As all interaction has moved online, maintaining and strengthening engagement with community members has been a challenge for Common Ground.

Solution: We identified key areas of the website that had the greatest opportunity to enable Common Ground to engage the existing community as a whole as well as attract new visitors.

The Team: Megan Pekarek, Noah Kaufmann, Andrea Anderson. UX/UI Designers

Tools: Adobe XD, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Invision, Miro

About Common Ground

Founded in 1993 Currently located in South Minneapolis

In 2009 a $900,000 purchase and renovation of new building

Over 500 visitors in the building per week

“People come to Common Ground Mediation Center for one thing, and find another”

Gabe Keller Flores

Common Ground Office Manager

Generally Common Ground visitors find other programs through the physical bulletin board at the center. With everything moving online due to covid, they have to make their offerings more discoverable online.

User Personas

Interview Process

To begin our research we conducted and recorded interviews with potential users over Zoom, took a survey among 27 participants, and held a stakeholder interview with the office manager of Common Ground.

In order to reflect the attendees of Common Ground, our participants ages ranged from 20 to 73. We looked at participant comfort levels with technology and their relationships with meditation and Buddhism.

Stakeholder Interview Insights

"The number one thing the I'd like the site to do that it's not doing now is get more people to join the newsletter"

Want there to be a "better walkthrough" for new meditators on the site

The calendar could have better sorting and be more user friendly in general. It is the heart of Common Ground.

Wish the site was less cluttered and had a seamless, elegant feel to it. Will continue online programming after Covid is over.

Feature Prioritization

Card Sorting

LoFi Prototype Testing

Asked four users to sign up for the newsletter. None of the users saw the newsletter link above the hamburger menu options.

Iterated LoFi Prototype

Because of how important the newsletter was to our stakeholder, we decided to make “Join the Newsletter” a feature button on the homepage.

HiFi Prototype Testing

  • “Oh, I had no idea I could scroll”
  • “I wasn’t sure if the flower was a logo or not, usually it’s on the left if it takes you home”
  • “The text is too large”

Iterated HiFi Prototype

  • Made content beneath fold peak up so users knew to scroll
  • Moved logo to the left to make it more obvious that it’s a home button
  • Reduced text size
